    Section Limits and Continuity - Chapter Exercises

    This section contains exercises related to limits and continuity. These exercises are designed to reinforce the concepts covered in the chapter and provide practice with various types of limit evaluations, continuity analysis, and applications of these concepts in different contexts.
    🔗

    Checkpoint 1. Graphical Limit Analysis.

    Referencing the provided graph of \(f(x)\text{,}\) determine which of the following statements are true. Explain your answers.
    🔗
    1. Does \(\lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x)\) exist?
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Does \(\lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x)\) exist?
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Does \(\lim_{x \to 2} f(x)\) exist?
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Is \(f\) continuous at \(x = 2\text{?}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    5. Does \(\lim_{x \to 4^-} f(x)\) exist?
      🔗
      🔗
    6. Does \(\lim_{x \to 4^+} f(x)\) exist?
      🔗
      🔗
    7. Does \(\lim_{x \to 4} f(x)\) exist?
      🔗
      🔗
    8. Is \(f\) continuous at \(x = 4\text{?}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    Solution.
    Based on the piecewise behavior described in the TikZ/Picture code:
    1. Yes. As \(x \to 2^-\text{,}\) \(f(x)\) approaches \(2\text{.}\)

      🔗
    2. Yes. As \(x \to 2^+\text{,}\) \(f(x)\) approaches \(3\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. No. The left-hand limit (\(2\)) and right-hand limit (\(3\)) are not equal.
      🔗
      🔗
    4. No. Continuity requires the limit to exist.
      🔗
      🔗
    5. Yes. As \(x \to 4^-\text{,}\) \(f(x)\) approaches \(3\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗

      🔗

      🔗

    Checkpoint 2. Continuity of Rational Functions.

    At what points is the function \(\displaystyle y = \frac{x + 5}{x^2 - 8x + 15}\) continuous?
    🔗
    Solution.
    A rational function is continuous everywhere except where the denominator is zero. Factor the denominator: \(x^2 - 8x + 15 = (x - 3)(x - 5)\text{.}\) The function is continuous on \((-\infty, 3) \cup (3, 5) \cup (5, \infty)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Checkpoint 3. Average Rate of Change.

    Find the average rate of change of \(f(x) = x^3 + 1\) over the interval \([-1, 1]\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Solution.
    The average rate of change is \(\frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} \frac{f(1) - f(-1)}{1 - (-1)} = \frac{(1^3 + 1) - ((-1)^3 + 1)}{2} = \frac{2 - 0}{2} = 1 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Checkpoint 4. Mixed Limit Evaluations.

    Find the following limits if they exist:
    🔗
    1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{3x^2 - 2x + 5}{2x^2 + 3x - 5}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 3^-} \frac{5}{x - 3}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    Solution.
    1. Multiply by conjugate: \(\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{x}-2}{(\sqrt{x}-2)(\sqrt{x}+2)} = \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}+2} = \frac{1}{4}\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Highest power of \(x\) is \(x^2\text{.}\) The limit is the ratio of coefficients: \(3/2\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. As \(x \to 3\) from the left, \(x - 3\) is a very small negative number. Thus, the limit is \(-\infty\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Checkpoint 5. Piecewise Continuity.

    For what value of \(b\) is \(g(x) = \begin{cases} x \amp x \lt -2 \\ bx^2 \amp x \ge -2 \end{cases}\) continuous at every \(x\text{?}\)
    🔗
    Solution.
    For continuity at \(x = -2\text{,}\) the one-sided limits must be equal:
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to -2^-} x = -2 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to -2^+} bx^2 = b(-2)^2 = 4b \end{equation*}
    Set them equal: \(4b = -2 \implies b = -1/2\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Checkpoint 6. Kinematics: Velocity and Acceleration.

    A body moves such that \(s = f(t) = -4.9t^2 + 15t + 30\text{.}\) Find the speed and acceleration at \(t = 2\) seconds.
    🔗
    Solution.
    1. Velocity: \(v(t) = s'(t) = -9.8t + 15\text{.}\) At \(t=2\text{,}\) \(v(2) = -19.6 + 15 = -4.6\) m/s. Speed is \(4.6\) m/s.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Acceleration: \(a(t) = v'(t) = -9.8\) m/s\(^2\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
